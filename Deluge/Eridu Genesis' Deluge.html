Eridu Genesis' Deluge

Eridu Genesis' Deluge

    The Creator Goddess Thinks About Humankind

    [Nintur was paying attention:]
  1. Let me bethink myself of my humankind, all forgotten as they are;
  2. and mindful of mine, Nintur's, creatures let me bring them back,
  3. let me lead the people back from their trails.
  4. Let they come and build cities and cult places,
  5. that I may cool myself in their shade;
  6. may they lay the bricks for the cult cities in pure spots,
  7. and may they found places for divination in pure spots!"
  8. She gave directions for purification, and cries for clemency,
  9. the things that cool divine wrath,
  10. perfected the divine service and the august offices,
  11. said to the surrounding regions: "Let me institute peace there!"
  12. When An, Enlil, Enki, and Ninhursaga
  13. fashioned the dark-headed people,
  14. they had made the small animals that came up from out of the earth
  15. come from the earth in abundance and had let there be, as befits it,
  16. gazelles, wild donkeys, and four-footed beasts in the desert.

      17. Creation of kingship

    1. ... "and let me have him advise;
    2. let me have him oversee their labor,
    3. and let him teach the nation to follow like unerringly like cattle!"
    4. When the royal scepter was coming down from heaven,
    5. the august crown and the royal throne being already down from heaven,
    6. the king regularly performed to perfection
    7. the august divine services and offices,
    8. and laid the bricks of those cities in pure spots.
    9. They were named by name and allotted half-bushel baskets.

        10. The first cities

      1. The firstling of the cities, Eridu, she gave to the leader Nudimmud,
      2. the second, Bad-Tibira, she gave to the Prince and the Sacred One,
      3. the third, Larak, she gave to Pahilsag,
      4. the fourth, Sippar, she gave to the gallant Utu,
      5. the fifth, Å uruppak, she gave to Ansud.
      6. These cities, which had been named by names,
      7. and had been alloted half-bushel baskets,
      8. dredged the canals, which were blocked with purplish
      9. wind-borne clay, and they carried water,
      10. Their cleaning of the canals established abundant growth

        Enki Intervenes

      1. That day, Nintur wept over her creatures
      2. And holy Inanna was full of grief over her people.
      3. But Enki took counsel with his own heart.
      4. An, Enlil, Enki, and Ninhursaga
      5. had the gods of heaven and earth swear by the names of An and Enlil.

        6. Ziusudra's Vision

      6. At that time Ziusudra was king and lustration priest.
      7. He fashioned, being a seer, the god of giddiness
      8. and stood in awe beside it, wording his wishes humbly.
      9. As he stood there regularly day after day,
      10. Something that was not a dream was appearing: conversation,
      11. A swearing of oaths by heaven and earth, a touching of throats,
      12. And the gods bringing their thwarts up to Kiur.

        13. Enki's advice

      13. And as Ziusudra stood there beside it, he went on hearing:
      14. "Step up to the wall to my left and listen!
      15. Let me speak a word to you at the wall and may you grasp what I say.
      16. May you heed my advice! By our hand a flood will sweep over
      17. the cities of the half-bushel baskets, and the country.
      18. The decision that mankind is to be destroyed has been made.
      19. A verdict, a command of the assembly, can not be revoked.
      20. No order of An and Enlil is known to have been countermanded.
      21. Their kingship, their term, has been uprooted; they must bethink themselves.
      22. Now listen
      23. What I have said to you."

        24. The Flood

      1. All the evil winds, all stormy winds gathered into
      2. one and with them. The Flood was sweeping over the cities of the half-bushel baskets,
      3. for seven days and seven nights.
      4. After the flood had swept over the country,
      5. after the evil wind had tossed the big boat about on the great waters,
      6. the sun came out spreading light over heaven and earth.

        7. Ziusudra's sacrifice

      7. Ziusudra then drilled an opening in the big boat
      8. and the gallant Utu sent his light into the interior of the big boat.

      1. Ziusudra, being the king,
      2. stepped up before Utu kissing the ground before him.
      3. The king was butchering oxen, was being lavish with the sheep,
      4. barley cakes, crescents together with victuals
      5. This he was serving for him
      7. With juniper, the pure plant of the mountains he filled on the fire
      8. and with a hand clasped to
      9. the breast he gave thanks.

        10. End of Enki's speech

      1. "You here have sworn by the life's breath of heaven, the life's breath of earth that he verily is allied with you yourself.
      2. You there, An and Enlil, have sworn by the life's breath of heaven, the life's breath of earth, that he is allies with all of you.
      3. He will disembark the small animals that come up from the earth!"

        4. Reward of Ziusudra

      4. Ziusudra, being king, stepped up before An and Enlil, kissing the ground,
      5. and An and Enlil after honoring him
      6. were granting life like a god's,
      7. were making lasting breath of life, like a god's, descend into him.
      8. That day they made Ziusudra, preserver, as king,
      9. of the small animals and the seed of mankind,
      10. live toward the east over the mountains of Dilmun.
