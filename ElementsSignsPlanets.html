The Lorentz Transformations attempted to reconcile the belief in the motion of earth with the findings of the Michelson-Morley experiment, with Einstein later taking the Lorentz Transformations as the basis of the Special Theory of Relativity. By the early 21st century, the failure to demonstrate the Special Theory in conjuction with the numerous Lorentz violations has helped fuel the crisis of physics.

Antimony

Antimony is a semi-metallic chemical element which can exist in two forms: the metallic form is bright, silvery, hard and brittle; the non-metallic form is a grey powder.  It is sometimes found free in nature, but is usually obtained from two ores, stibnite 🜫 and valentinite.  Antimony makes up about 0.00002% of the earth's crust.

The metal antimony symbolises the animal nature or wild spirit of man and nature and was often depicted by the wolf.

Arsenic

Arsenic 🜺 is represented by the image of a swan, and a variety of seemingly unrelated symbols have been used to represent the element.  Several forms involved a cross and then two circles or an 'S' shape.

Arsenic was a well-known poison, so the swan symbol might not make much sense until you consider the fact that the element is a metalloid.  Like other elements in the group, arsenic can transform from one physical appearance to another.  These allotropes display different properties from each other.  Cygnets turn into swans; arsenic, too, transforms itself.

Atomic Number: 33
